Social media has always been the home of thriving niche communities, but it’s never been more important for brands to nurture these spaces. According to Sprout’s The State of Social Media Report, community-focused content is the second most-desired content type from users today, while 51% expect brands to more actively engage with their audiences on all platforms.
Social media community management represents a unique opportunity for brands, but it does come with hurdles. Where more traditional types of community management (like hosting events or handling customer service complaints) allow brands control, on social, you must adapt to the engagement standards of each community and social network.
That means working across different platforms, managing multiple conversations at once and staying up-to-date on network updates and trends. In today’s new social media landscape, engagement is just as (if not even more) important as entertainment. Active social media communities build hype around a brand, providing organic amplification for launches and promotional activities.
In this article, we share how you can create more impactful audience experiences through community management on social media.
What is social media community management?

Social media community management is the process of engaging audiences across social media networks to increase brand loyalty and grow authentic connections. This function oversees the one-to-one or one-to-few interactions that strengthen relationships and promote brand advocacy.
Nearly every social media network—Facebook, Instagram, X (formerly known as Twitter), LinkedIn, Twitch, Reddit, Threads—has features that support creating and maintaining active communities. A social media community management strategy defines the cross-network approach a business takes when managing conversations with brand advocates.
That said, not all communities are created equal. You should refine tactics based on where your community lives to yield the best results.
What is a social media community manager?
Social media community managers are responsible for engaging audiences across networks to further develop a community. At a high level, this may sound like a social media manager’s responsibility, but there are distinct differences that separate the two roles.
For example, you might’ve seen Oatly’s former Social Engagement & Community Growth Specialist, Paula Perez, in an Oatly TikTok from time to time. However, she wasn’t part of content planning.
“I’ll star in a TikTok on occasion because I’m comfortable in front of a camera, but I don’t plan or create any of the content that goes on our social media profiles,” Perez told us during a previous interview. “Our creative and social teams work together on that.”
